Dhamma Vinaya Monastery: A Path to Universal Peace and Liberation.

Welcome to Dhamma Vinaya Monastery Pune (DVMP), a 10-acre sanctuary in the serene landscapes of Agalambe near Khadakwasla. we are dedicated to preserving the Buddha’s original teachings—Dhamma (the Truth) and Vinaya (the Discipline).

Key Highlights include:

  • Holy Relics: Home to a grand Sanchi-style Stupa housing the sacred relics of the Buddha.
  • Universal Reach: A center envisioned by Most Venerable Arayawangso Guruji to guide all humanity.
  • Nature-Aligned: Adjoined by the 100-acre Veluvan Meditation Park.

About Us

Our Mission: To provide a dedicated space for the study and practice of Buddhist monastic discipline and meditation. We believe that when Dhamma and Vinaya function together, they lead to total unbinding and release from suffering.

The center has been developed by the Bahujan Hitay Samajik Bandhilki Sanstha (BHSBS) Pune, the result of years of dedication by its Trustee's. The first phase was inaugurated on the 2587th Buddha Jayanti (May 2023) by Thai Buddhist scholar Most Ven. Arayawangso Guruji.

Our Architecture

  • The Grand Stupa: A replica of the Great Stupa at Sanchi, serving as a beacon of faith. Unlike the Sanchi Stupa it is hollow from inside and has two spacious meditation halls of 2700sqft each. A perfect space for reflection, contemplation, and meditation.
  • Meditation & Uposatha Halls: Spacious halls designed for collective meditation and monastic ceremonies.
  • Bhikkhu & Sadhaka Niwas: Secluded residential quarters for monks and practitioners to live and meditate in peace.
  • Administrative Building: The hub for managing monastery activities and course registrations.
  • Gypsy Vipassana Center: Part of the 10 acre complex nearing completion, It has 108 rooms and 3 large halls. 10 days Vipassana meditation courses will be arranged soon, apart from Skill development, Womens empowerment, Cadre development courses.

Meet the Monks

Our spiritual community is led by the esteemed Abbott Arayawangso Guruji, who has guided us on the path of mindfulness and compassion for the last decade.

Visiting Information

We welcome visitors from all walks of life. The monastery is open for tours and meditation retreats. Below are the general visiting hours:

  • Monday - Friday: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
  • Saturday - Sunday: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM

Guidelines for visitors:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Maintain silence in meditation halls.
  • Respect the monks and their practices.
