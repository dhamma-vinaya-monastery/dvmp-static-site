Dhamma Vinaya Monastery: A Path to Universal Peace and Liberation.

Welcome to Dhamma Vinaya Monastery Pune (DVMP), a 10-acre sanctuary in the serene landscapes of Agalambe near Khadakwasla. We are dedicated to preserving the Buddha’s original teachings—Dhamma (the Truth) and Vinaya (the Discipline).

Key Highlights include:

  • Holy Relics: Home to a grand Sanchi-style Stupa housing the sacred relics of the Buddha and Arahants, gifted by the Thai Sangha.
  • Universal Reach: A center envisioned by Most Venerable Arayawangso Guruji to guide all humanity.
  • Nature-Aligned: Adjoined by the 100-acre Veluvan Meditation Park.

About Us

Our Mission: To provide a dedicated space for the study and practice of Buddhist monastic discipline and meditation. We believe that when Dhamma and Vinaya function together, they lead to total unbinding and release from suffering.

The center has been developed by the Bahujan Hitay Samajik Bandhilki Sanstha (BHSBS) Pune, the result of years of dedication by its trustees. The first phase was inaugurated on the 2587th Buddha Jayanti (May 2023) by Thai Buddhist scholar Most Ven. Arayawangso Guruji.

Programs & Activities:
  • Mega Dhamma Courses: Enriching residential programs led by esteemed monks, such as Ven. Ananda Bhante ji (Mahabodhi Society, Bangalore)
  • One-Day Retreats: Regular one-day Dhamma camps for local and visiting practitioners.
  • Meditation Practice: Daily sessions focusing on Anapana and Vipassana for self-transformation and mental purification.
  • Ceremonial Events: Annual celebrations including Rains Retreat (Vassa), Kathin Chivardan and Buddha Jayanti.

Our Architecture

  • The Grand Stupa: A replica of the Great Stupa at Sanchi, serving as a beacon of faith. Unlike the Sanchi Stupa it is hollow from inside and has two spacious meditation halls of 2700sqft each. A perfect space for reflection, contemplation, and meditation.
  • Meditation & Uposatha Halls: Spacious halls designed for collective meditation and monastic ceremonies.
  • Bhikkhu & Sadhaka Niwas: Secluded residential quarters for monks and practitioners to live and meditate in peace.
  • Administrative Building: The hub for managing monastery activities and course registrations.
  • Gypsy Vipassana Center: Part of the 10 acre complex nearing completion. It has 108 rooms and 3 large halls. 10 days Vipassana meditation courses will be arranged soon, apart from Skill development, women’s empowerment, cadre development courses.
  • Veluvan Meditation Park: A sacred forest meditation space is currently being developed. Spread across approximately 100 acres of natural landscape, the park draws inspiration from the ancient Veluvana tradition—the bamboo groves offered to the Buddha for meditation—and embodies a commitment to simplicity, harmony, and contemplative living. Initiated under the guidance of Most Venerable Arayawangso Guruji, the project began with the planting of a Bodhi tree and continues to take shape as a serene environment dedicated to meditation, mindfulness, and the preservation of nature. Envisioned as a quiet refuge for spiritual practice, Veluvan Meditation Park will feature forest pathways, silent meditation areas, and spaces that support inner stillness and a deeper connection with the Dhamma.

Meet the Monks

Most Venerable Arayawangso is a world-renowned meditation master and a senior figure in the Thai Forest Tradition. As the Abbot of both Buddhapojhariphunchai Monastery (Lamphun, Thailand) and Dhamma Vinaya Monastery (Pune, India), he serves as a spiritual bridge between the two nations.

Known for his strict adherence to monastic discipline and his expertise in deep meditation, "Guruji" has dedicated his life to reviving the Buddhist heritage of South Asia. His notable contributions include:

  • Global Peace Ambassador: Leading "Bell of Peace" ceremonies across India and Pakistan to promote interfaith harmony.
  • Dharma Education: Guiding international practitioners through intensive retreats and the study of the Pāli Canon.
  • Cultural Diplomacy: Strengthening ties between Thailand and India through high-level Buddhist summits and Samvad initiatives.

Under his guidance, Dhamma Vinaya Monastery Pune serves as a sanctuary for those seeking authentic Dhamma practice and mental peace in the modern world.

Support Our Mission

In the teachings of the Buddha, dāna (generosity) is the foundation of spiritual practice. It is through giving—freely and selflessly—that one cultivates compassion, reduces attachment, and supports the continuation of the Dhamma in the world.

Dhamma Seva: We encourage students to offer their time in service. Serving others in the Dhamma is considered deeply beneficial for one’s own spiritual growth and helps sustain the monastic community.

Generosity (Dāna): This project is built and maintained entirely through the voluntary contributions of Dhamma practitioners and well-wishers. Your support ensures that the teachings remain accessible and freely available to all.

Dhamma Vinaya Ratna (DVR): To support the recurring expenses of the monastery and its social initiatives, individuals who contribute ₹1000 per month on a regular basis are honored as Dhamma Vinaya Ratna, recognizing their ongoing commitment to sustaining the Dhamma.

Your donations help sustain the monastery, support our monks, and fund meditation retreats and spiritual courses. Thank you for your kindness.
